Clay Soil

Drainage: Poor
Fertility: High
pH Range: 6.0-7.0
Water Retention: Excellent

Key Characteristics:

  • Fine particles, sticky when wet
  • Rich in nutrients and minerals
  • Slow to warm up in spring
  • Can become waterlogged

Best Crops:

Rice Wheat Sugarcane Cotton

Sandy Soil

Drainage: Excellent
Fertility: Low
pH Range: 5.5-6.5
Water Retention: Poor

Key Characteristics:

  • Large particles, easy to work with
  • Warms up quickly in spring
  • Good aeration and drainage
  • Requires frequent watering

Best Crops:

Tomatoes Carrots Potatoes Onions

Loamy Soil

Drainage: Good
Fertility: Excellent
pH Range: 6.0-7.5
Water Retention: Good

Key Characteristics:

  • Perfect balance of clay, sand, and silt
  • Easy to work with and fertile
  • Good water retention and drainage
  • Ideal for most crops

Best Crops:

Corn Beans Lettuce Most Vegetables

Silty Soil

Drainage: Moderate
Fertility: High
pH Range: 6.0-7.0
Water Retention: Good

Key Characteristics:

  • Smooth texture, retains moisture
  • Rich in organic matter
  • Can become compacted when wet
  • Prone to erosion

Best Crops:

Strawberries Grass Bamboo Willow

Peaty Soil

Drainage: Poor
Fertility: Moderate
pH Range: 3.5-5.0
Water Retention: Excellent

Key Characteristics:

  • High organic matter content
  • Acidic in nature
  • Retains moisture well
  • Slow to warm up

Best Crops:

Blueberries Azaleas Heather Cranberries

Chalky Soil

Drainage: Excellent
Fertility: Moderate
pH Range: 7.5-8.5
Water Retention: Poor

Key Characteristics:

  • Alkaline soil with limestone
  • Free-draining, shallow depth
  • Can be stony and rocky
  • Prone to drying out

Best Crops:

Spinach Cabbage Sweet Corn Lilac
